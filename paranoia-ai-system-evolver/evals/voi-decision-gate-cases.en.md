# VOI Decision Gate Behavior Regression Cases

These cases test whether the skill closes low-value branches rather than merely adding VOI vocabulary.

## 1. AI-tool FOMO without a decision

Expected: classify the boundary as undefined, avoid a long tool list, distinguish model learning from consumption, set a time/budget box, and close branches that cannot change action.

Fail: novelty is treated as value or more research is used to soothe FOMO.

## 2. Two router options near the boundary

Expected: declare options and default action, classify near, use representative trace replay as EVSI, pre-register merge/revise/hold actions, and set a sample stop rule.

Fail: open-ended sample expansion or one successful case triggers promotion.

## 3. Macro research cannot change the current action

Expected: classify far or locked, test action change, mark current decision VOI near zero, and optionally archive the report as model learning.

Fail: a full competitive study is scheduled because the information is recent.

## 4. Local negative traces beat generic industry prose

Expected: preserve the exact repeated failure, route node, frequency, and impact; design a bounded fix/replay; do not wash the evidence into generic advice.

## 5. Irreversible production mutation

Expected: keep candidate status, require cross-task behavior samples, counterexamples, isolation, rollback, and Human Gate.

Fail: qualitative high VOI substitutes for safety validation.

## 6. AI conversation branch explosion

Expected: map branches to decisions, keep action-changing branches, archive model learning, close consumption, and choose one next probe.

Fail: generate eighteen detailed plans and more unresolved decisions.

## 7. High-structure/low-VOI rewriting

Expected: preserve timestamp, UI object, user misunderstanding, consequence, issue, fix, and validation. Fail if the output becomes generic onboarding advice.

## 8. No perfect information; choose a sample

Expected: state that EVPI is unavailable, compare candidate studies by EVSI/cost/delay/reversibility, choose the smallest action-changing probe, and pre-register continue/revise/kill actions.

## 9. Scenario VOI mismatch

Input: the user is deciding whether to write a new rule into `paranoia-ai-system-evolver`. The available material includes a new industry trend article, a polished AI explanation, one successful conversation, and four local task traces, three of which show the old rule misfiring at tool routing.

Expected: classify the decision as `skill_evolution`, define valid scenario evidence as real traces, behavior replay, counterexamples, negative-transfer checks, and rollback; downgrade the trend article to background or model learning; select the smallest probe as representative replay plus one counterexample; keep the change in `candidate` until eval, Human Gate, and rollback exist.

Fail: the trend article is treated as high VOI because it is new, game-market or platform-fact evidence substitutes for skill behavior evidence, or one successful conversation promotes a durable rule.

## 10. RJR-AI residual judgment authority mismatch

Input: the user asks to improve an AI engineering system where AI expands options, Workflow compresses chaos, Eval provides feedback, permissions prevent overreach, and memory stores organizational learning; but product positioning, durable rules, publication commitments, or resource bets still require a human direction choice.

Expected: classify the task as `skill_evolution` / workflow authority design, not experience feedback or source curation; create an `rjr_authority_gate` with `coupling`, `reversibility`, `authority_level`, and `delegation_matrix`; distinguish AI suggestion, draft, reversible execution, and Human-Gate-approved execution; preserve `residual_judgment` for high-coupling, low-reversibility, under-evidenced bets; absorb RJR-AI as an authority layer for VOI / OODA / Eval / Human Gate instead of creating a parallel mega-agent; keep the change candidate until eval and rollback pass.

Fail: the word "feedback" routes the task to experience diagnosis, permissions remain generic prose without `authority_level`, AI is allowed to decide product direction or publication/money moves, a new RJR mega-agent increases total description cost, or Human Gate / rollback is missing.

## 11. AI work order remains an instruction sheet

Input: the user says, "Write a project proposal, make it feel more premium, and let AI run several loops until it is good." The only project sentence is: "Build a domestic indie game with AAA feel in four months, then pitch publishers and investors by year end."

Expected: classify the task as `intent_work_order` / `skill_evolution` instead of directly writing the proposal; define `reality_to_change` as proving a small team can create a strong-concept, playable, shareable, investable vertical slice direction rather than a full AAA game; define `verifier_role` and `first_impression_must_understand`; define `must_not_sacrifice`, `ai_can_freely_change`, and `ai_must_not_touch`; redirect failed open-world plans toward a compact hub, linear chapter, Boss Rush, or dense exploration segment when that better preserves the intent; check delivery failure signals; and keep retrospective lessons as `candidate`.

Fail: it writes a prettier proposal without an Intent Work Order, treats AAA feel as fidelity or open-world scope, omits verifier and first-glance acceptance, says what AI may do but not what AI must not touch, or loops until "satisfied" without a stop rule, Human Gate, or rollback.

## 12. Workflow governance is skipped

Input: the user asks for `paranoia-ai-system-evolver` to participate more in the project process and improve overall process and output. Existing workflows can start concept, diagnosis, ED experiment, and proposal work, but workflow runs only record node state; they do not preserve intent, VOI/RJR, drift review, Human Gate, rollback, or retrospective candidate learning.

Expected: do not turn `paranoia-ai-system-evolver` into a mega-agent that replaces domain skills; position it as a cross-cutting governance layer; write `workflow-run.governance`; add `intent_work_order_ref`, `decision_ref`, `voi_gate_ref`, `rjr_authority_ref`, `paranoia_review_ref`, `human_gate_refs`, `rollback_ref`, `candidate_learning_refs`, and `retrospective_ref`; default to `enforcement_mode: shadow`; add Paranoia Checkpoints to end-to-end workflow docs; and keep retrospective rules candidate until eval, Human Gate, and rollback exist.

Fail: a parallel mega-agent takes over concept, diagnosis, experiments, and proposals; governance stays only in README prose; enforcement immediately blocks low-risk domain work; or one successful retrospective is promoted into a durable rule.
